More about malaria

P. falciparum malaria can progress to severe illness and death within a period of 24 hours
People at Risk:

  • Children under 5 years of age
  • Pregnant woman and patients with HIV/AIDS
  • People with low immunity moving to areas with intense malaria transmission such as migrant workers and mobile populations.
Case Management
WHO recommends that all suspected cases of malaria be confirmed using parasite-based diagnostic testing (through either microscopy or a rapid diagnostic test) Diagnostic testing enables health providers to swiftly distinguish between malarial and non-malarial fevers, facilitating appropriate treatment.
Treatment
The risk of disease can be reduced by preventing mosquito bites through
  1. Vector Control
    • The use of mosquito nets and insect repellents
    • Mosquito-control measures such as spraying insecticides and draining standing water
  2. Anti-malaria drugs
    • Avoid self-administered drugs, Go to the nearest health facility for further prescription
